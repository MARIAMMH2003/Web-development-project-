Luxor Museum


Unveiling Ancient Treasures Anew


28th May 1975


The Luxor Museum, founded in 1975, stands as a beacon of Egypt's rich cultural heritage. Nestled on the East Bank of the Nile, it boasts a stunning collection of artifacts primarily from the Theban Necropolis and Luxor Temple. Showcasing a fusion of ancient Egyptian and Greco-Roman influences, the museum offers a captivating journey through millennia of history.
Visitors are greeted by a meticulously curated display featuring statues of pharaohs, intricately carved reliefs, and exquisite artifacts unearthed from nearby archaeological sites. Noteworthy highlights include the renowned granite colossus of Amenhotep III and the beautifully preserved mummy of Ramses I.
The museum's design, with its airy galleries and expansive windows, provides a serene backdrop for contemplation and exploration. Each artifact tells a story of ancient rituals, royal power, and artistic mastery, offering glimpses into the lives of the pharaohs and their subjects.
Beyond its permanent collection, the Luxor Museum hosts temporary exhibitions and educational programs, engaging visitors of all ages with the wonders of ancient Egypt. Whether admiring the delicate craftsmanship of a golden amulet or pondering the mysteries of an inscribed stele, every visit to the Luxor Museum promises a deeper understanding of this timeless civilization.
