The Smithsonian Institution, founded in 1846, stands as the world's
largest museum, education, and research complex, comprising 19 museums, galleries,
gardens, and a zoo. Nestled primarily in Washington D.C., its diverse collections span
art, history, culture, science, and technology, attracting millions of visitors annually.

The National Museum of American History showcases the nation's cultural heritage, featuring
iconic artifacts such as the Star-Spangled Banner and the First Ladies' inaugural gowns.
The National Air and Space Museum chronicles humanity's quest for the skies, housing the Wright
brothers' plane and the Apollo 11 command module.

In the heart of the National Mall, the National Museum of Natural History unveils the mysteries
of our planet and its inhabitants, including the Hope Diamond and dinosaur fossils. Meanwhile,
the National Portrait Gallery immortalizes significant figures through portraiture, offering a
visual narrative of American history and culture.

Beyond D.C., the Smithsonian's reach extends across the country, with facilities
like the National Museum of African American History and Culture in Harlem and the National
Museum of the American Indian in New York City. These institutions stand as beacons of knowledge and
inspiration, fostering curiosity, understanding, and appreciation for the world's wonders.
