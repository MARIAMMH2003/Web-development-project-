Alexandria National Museum


Echoes of Alexandria's Glory


31st December 2003


The Alexandria National Museum, founded in 2003, stands as a testament to the city's
rich and diverse history. Situated in a stunning Italianate-style building, it serves as a
treasure trove of artifacts, spanning various epochs and civilizations. From ancient
Egyptian relics to artifacts from the Greco-Roman period, Islamic art,
and Coptic treasures, the museum offers a comprehensive
journey through Alexandria's past.

Visitors can immerse themselves in the maritime history of the city, exploring
exhibits on Alexandria's significance as a hub of trade and culture. The museum also
showcases the architectural wonders of Alexandria, highlighting its role as a melting pot
of different cultures and influences.

Through its carefully curated exhibits, the Alexandria National Museum provides insights
into the city's vibrant past, celebrating its cultural heritage and its contributions to human
civilization. Whether you're interested in ancient history, art, or archaeology, the museum offers
something for everyone, making it a must-visit destination for anyone seeking to explore the rich tapestry of
Alexandria's history.

The Alexandria National Museum houses a collection of Egyptian sarcophagi, which were used to encase and preserve the bodies of the deceased in ancient Egypt. These elaborately decorated coffins often depict scenes from Egyptian mythology and religious beliefs, offering valuable insights into ancient Egyptian culture, burial practices, and beliefs about the afterlife. The sarcophagi serve as important artifacts for studying the funerary customs and religious beliefs of ancient Egypt.
