British Museum


Unveiling Humanity's Tapestry.


7th June 1753


The British Museum, established on June 7, 1753, is a cultural treasure trove housing over eight
million artifacts from every corner of the globe. Its expansive collections span diverse civilizations
and epochs, offering visitors a captivating journey through human history and creativity.

From the Rosetta Stone to the Elgin Marbles, the museum's iconic exhibits represent pivotal moments in
world history and highlight the achievements of ancient civilizations. Visitors can explore the Egyptian
mummies, marvel at Greek sculptures, and study the intricate artifacts of Mesopotamia, each offering insights into
the beliefs, customs, and technologies of bygone eras.

Beyond its renowned collections, the British Museum serves as a hub for research, education, and cultural exchange.
Scholars and experts from around the world converge here to study and interpret its vast holdings, contributing to our
collective understanding of human civilization.

Through innovative exhibitions, lectures, and educational programs, the museum engages visitors of all ages and
backgrounds, fostering a deeper appreciation for global heritage and promoting cross-cultural dialogue. Its commitment
to accessibility ensures that the museum remains a welcoming space for all, enriching lives and inspiring curiosity.

As a custodian of cultural heritage, the British Museum plays a vital role in preserving the legacy of human
achievement and promoting cultural understanding and appreciation. With its enduring legacy and global significance,
it continues to inspire and educate millions, shaping our understanding of the past and informing our vision for the future.

Famous Monuments



The Rosetta Stone, discovered in 1799, is a key artifact in understanding ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs. Inscribed with the same decree in three scripts—hieroglyphic, demotic, and Greek—it provided the key to deciphering Egyptian hieroglyphs. Housed in the British Museum, it remains a symbol of linguistic breakthrough and cultural exchange.

The Gayer-Anderson cat is a bronze figure depicting one form of the goddess Bastet. The goddess was usually shown as a cat-headed woman, or in the form of a cat. Her principal cult centre was Bubastis in the Nile Delta. Bastet was a mother goddess and benign counterpart to the more aggressive lion goddess Sekhmet.

Brass head of an Ooni (king) of Ife, Nigeria 1300s - early 1400s This crowned head was discovereg by accident in 1939 during digging close to the royal palace in Ife. Between 1100 and 1400 Ife was one of the great city-states in West Africa and flourished as a commercial centre within regional and long-distance trade networks..

The goddess Tara, found between Trincomalee and Batticaloa, Sri Lanka, made AD 700- 750 This bronze statue of Buddhist goddess Tara was found on the east coast of Sri Lanka.

Ancient Greek black-figured amphora (wine jar), dated 540-530 BC Signed by Exekias as potter and Attributed to him as painter. The scene on this vase shows Achilles looming above Amazon warrior Penthesilea as she sinks to the ground.
